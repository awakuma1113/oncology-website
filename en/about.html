Department Overview

Supporting patients through team medicine that transcends departmental boundaries based on the latest knowledge

Role of Medical Oncology

Medical Oncology is a department that primarily treats cancer using pharmacotherapy.

We treat advanced cancers that are difficult to manage with local therapies such as surgery and radiation by combining chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and targeted therapy. We also act as coordinators for multidisciplinary treatment, collaborating with surgical and radiation departments as necessary to provide optimal care tailored to each patient.

Our Features and Goals

Our goal is to provide high-quality cancer pharmacotherapy by practicing team medicine that goes beyond the boundaries of specific clinical departments.

Cancer treatment is undergoing major changes with the introduction of targeted therapies and immune checkpoint inhibitors. Our aim is to provide these novel treatments appropriately while ensuring an environment where patients can live with peace of mind.

Multidisciplinary Team Care

Not only physicians, but a diverse team including board-certified oncology pharmacists, certified nurse specialists in cancer nursing, certified nurses in palliative care, medical social workers, and registered dietitians share their expertise in conferences to provide the best possible care aligned with the patient's own wishes.

In-Hospital Collaboration

We hold regular tumor boards (joint conferences) with organ-specific surgical departments, the radiation oncology department, diagnostic radiology, and pathology. Our department coordinates these efforts to provide optimal "multidisciplinary treatment" (the best combination of individual therapies) for every single patient.

History of Our Department

  • April 2013 Establishment of Tohoku Pharmaceutical University Hospital and the Department of Medical Oncology
  • April 2016 Hospital name changed to Tohoku Medical and Pharmaceutical University Hospital
  • January 2018 Organization of the Outpatient Chemotherapy Center and the Department of Clinical Genetics
  • April 2021 Designated as a Regional Cancer Care Hospital
  • July 2021 Opening of the Genetic Counseling Outpatient Clinic
  • December 2023 Designated as a Cooperative Hospital for Cancer Genomic Medicine
  • January 2025 Initiation of in-house cancer gene panel testing submissions
